# Netflix — Culture Deck + Keeper Test + Freedom & Responsibility

## Interview Format
- Typically 5-6 interviews, can stretch to 8+
- Heavy on culture fit — they screen aggressively for culture alignment
- Mix of: behavioral, role-specific, culture, leadership
- Often includes conversations with skip-level leaders

## The Culture Deck (Know This Cold)
Netflix's culture memo is public and defines everything:
- **Freedom & Responsibility** — high autonomy, high accountability
- **Context, Not Control** — leaders set context, not micromanage
- **Highly Aligned, Loosely Coupled** — strategic alignment, tactical freedom
- **Pay Top of Market** — they don't negotiate, they pay well upfront
- **Keeper Test** — "Would I fight to keep this person?" If no, generous severance

## What They Test

### Judgment
- "Tell me about a time you made a decision others disagreed with"
- "How do you decide what NOT to do?"
- They want independent thinkers who make smart bets
- Show you can operate without a playbook

### Courage
- "Tell me about a time you gave difficult feedback"
- "When did you disagree with your manager?"
- They value candor — direct, honest, even uncomfortable
- "Sunshining" = being transparent about problems

### Impact
- "What's the biggest business impact you've had?"
- They care about outcomes, not process or effort
- Quantify everything — revenue, users, efficiency gains
- Show you punch above your weight

### Curiosity
- "What are you learning right now?"
- "What's a recent trend in [domain] that most people are wrong about?"
- They hire people who are deeply curious about their domain

## Tips
- Read the Netflix culture memo before the interview — they will ask
  about it directly or indirectly
- No "we" stories — they want to know what YOU did specifically
- They reject "brilliant jerks" — high performance AND good collaboration
- Don't ask about work-life balance in a way that signals low intensity
- Show you thrive with autonomy and ambiguity
- Mention specific Netflix content/product decisions you found interesting
