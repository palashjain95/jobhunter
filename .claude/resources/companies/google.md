# Google — Product Sense + Analytical + Googliness

## Interview Format
- Typically 4-5 interviews
- Mix of: product sense, analytical, leadership, Googliness
- For PM roles: heavy on product design and metrics

## Product Sense Questions
"How would you improve [Google product]?"
"Design a product for [use case]."

### Structure
Clarify → Users → Pain Points → Solutions → Prioritize → Metrics

### Tips
- Always start by clarifying the goal and constraints
- Segment users before proposing solutions
- Prioritize using impact vs effort
- End with how you'd measure success (metrics)
- Think about edge cases and potential downsides

## Analytical Questions
"How many piano tuners are in Chicago?"
"Estimate the revenue of YouTube Premium."

### Structure
Define → Segment → Estimate → Calculate → Sanity Check

### Tips
- State assumptions clearly
- Break the problem into manageable segments
- Round numbers are fine — show your reasoning
- Sanity check at the end: "Does this number make sense?"

## Googliness (Culture Fit)
- Comfort with ambiguity
- Collaborative, not hierarchical
- Intellectual curiosity and humility
- "What's a strong opinion you hold?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to work with incomplete data"
- They want people who disagree respectfully and change their mind
  when presented with better data
