# McKinsey / BCG / Bain — PEI + Case Interview Framework

## Personal Experience Interview (PEI)

Different from standard behavioral. Three dimensions tested:

1. **Leadership** — mobilizing others, driving change
2. **Personal Impact** — influencing without authority
3. **Entrepreneurial Drive** — building something, taking initiative

### How to Structure PEI Answers
- Longer than standard STAR (3-5 minutes per story)
- Deep follow-ups expected: "What specifically did YOU do?"
- They probe for individual contribution, not team outcomes
- Practice going 3 levels deep on any story
- McKinsey wants ONE deep story per dimension, not multiple shallow ones

### McKinsey vs BCG vs Bain Differences
- **McKinsey:** Most structured. PEI is half the interview. Very formal.
- **BCG:** More conversational behavioral. Case is more creative.
- **Bain:** Behavioral + case. Values "true north" — ethical reasoning.

## Case Interview

### Standard Framework
Clarify → Structure → Prioritize → Analyze → Recommend

### Common Case Types
- Profitability: Revenue vs cost breakdown
- Market entry: Market size → competition → capabilities → economics
- Pricing: Value-based vs cost-plus vs competitive
- M&A: Strategic fit → financials → integration risks
- Operations: Bottleneck identification → root cause → solution

### Tips
- Use frameworks as starting points, not scripts
- Mental math matters — practice arithmetic without a calculator
- Recommendation must be actionable with clear next steps
- Structure your answer visually (talk through a framework tree)
- Ask clarifying questions before jumping in
- It's OK to take 30 seconds to think before answering
