# Private Equity — Deal Discussion + Behavioral Framework

## Interview Structure

Typical PE interview loop:
1. **Fit/Behavioral** — why PE, why this fund, why now (especially for MBA pivots)
2. **Deal Discussion** — walk through a deal you've worked on or studied
3. **Case Study / Paper LBO** — model a simplified buyout on paper
4. **Technical Finance** — valuation, accounting, capital structure questions
5. **Partner/Principal** — culture fit, long-term career vision, judgment calls

## Deal Discussion (Most Critical)

### Structure (5-7 minutes)
1. **Overview** — what the company does, why it was an attractive target
2. **Thesis** — why this deal made sense (growth, margin expansion, multiple expansion, add-ons)
3. **Due Diligence** — what you looked at, what risks you found
4. **Your Role** — what YOU specifically did (not the team)
5. **Outcome** — returns, what happened, what you'd do differently

### What They're Really Testing
- Can you think like an investor, not just an analyst?
- Do you understand what makes a good business?
- Can you identify risks and mitigate them?
- Are you intellectually honest about what went wrong?

### Common Follow-Ups
- "What would you pay for this business?" → demonstrate valuation intuition
- "What could go wrong?" → show risk awareness
- "Would you do this deal today?" → show market awareness
- "What was the most difficult part of diligence?" → show depth

## Paper LBO

### Framework (10-15 minutes)
- Sources & Uses (debt, equity, fees)
- 5-year projection (revenue growth, margin assumptions, capex)
- Debt paydown schedule
- Exit multiple assumption → exit equity value
- IRR calculation (back-of-envelope: 2x in 3 years ≈ 26%, 3x in 5 years ≈ 25%)

### Key Drivers to Articulate
- Revenue growth vs margin expansion vs multiple expansion vs debt paydown
- Which lever matters most for THIS deal?

## Technical Finance Questions

Common topics:
- Walk me through a DCF
- What happens to each financial statement when depreciation increases by $10?
- How does leverage affect returns in a buyout?
- What's the difference between enterprise value and equity value?
- When would you use a sum-of-parts valuation?

## Behavioral (PE-Specific)

### Questions That Differ from Standard Behavioral
- "Why PE and not banking / consulting / VC?" → show you understand the asset class
- "What do you do when a portfolio company is underperforming?" → judgment + accountability
- "How do you work with management teams?" → influence without direct authority
- "Describe a time you changed your mind on an investment" → intellectual honesty

### For MBA Candidates Specifically
- "What does your MBA add to this role?" → analytical frameworks + network + credibility
- "How do you go from [previous career] to PE?" → narrative coherence
- "What sector are you most interested in and why?" → show genuine conviction

## Tips
- PE interviews are more conversational than banking — show judgment, not just technical skill
- Have 2-3 deals ready: one you worked on, one you'd pitch, one that failed
- Know current market conditions: rates, multiples, dry powder, deal flow
- For MBA pivots: acknowledge the learning curve honestly, emphasize transferable pattern recognition
