# Finance (Corporate Finance, Investment Banking, Asset Management) — Interview Framework

## Interview Structure by Role

### Investment Banking
1. **Technical** — accounting, valuation, DCF, merger model, LBO basics
2. **Behavioral** — why banking, deal experience, teamwork under pressure
3. **Industry Knowledge** — sector-specific trends and recent deals
4. **Fit** — personality, work ethic, long hours acknowledgment

### Corporate Finance / FP&A
1. **Technical** — financial statements, budgeting, variance analysis, forecasting
2. **Behavioral** — cross-functional influence, simplifying complexity for non-finance stakeholders
3. **Case / Modeling** — build a simple model or analyze a business scenario
4. **Strategic** — how finance supports business decisions

### Asset Management / Buy-Side
1. **Stock Pitch** — present an investment thesis (long or short)
2. **Technical** — valuation, accounting, market mechanics
3. **Behavioral** — independent thinking, conviction under uncertainty
4. **Market Discussion** — current views on markets, sectors, risks

## Technical Finance Questions

### Accounting
- Walk me through the three financial statements and how they connect
- A company prepays $100 for insurance — walk through the impact on all three statements
- What's the difference between cash and accrual accounting?
- How does a stock buyback affect EPS?

### Valuation
- Walk me through a DCF (WACC, terminal value, sensitivity)
- When would you use comparable companies vs precedent transactions vs DCF?
- What drives enterprise value vs equity value?
- Company A trades at 15x EBITDA, Company B at 10x — what might explain the difference?

### M&A / Deals
- Walk me through an accretion/dilution analysis
- Why would a company choose debt vs equity to finance an acquisition?
- What synergies would you look for in [industry] M&A?
- Is this deal accretive? How would you assess that quickly?

## Stock Pitch Framework (Asset Management)

### Structure (5-10 minutes)
1. **Company overview** — what they do, business model, competitive position
2. **Thesis** — why buy/sell (2-3 specific catalysts)
3. **Valuation** — current vs intrinsic value, key assumptions
4. **Risks** — what could go wrong, how to mitigate
5. **Timeline** — when does the thesis play out?

### What They're Testing
- Do you think independently or recite consensus?
- Can you articulate risk vs reward?
- Is your thesis specific and testable, or vague?
- Do you know when you'd be wrong?

## Behavioral (Finance-Specific)

### Common Questions
- "Why finance?" → show genuine interest in markets/businesses, not just prestige
- "Tell me about a time you analyzed data that changed a decision" → analytical impact
- "How do you explain complex financial concepts to non-finance people?" → communication
- "Describe a mistake in an analysis and what you learned" → intellectual honesty
- "What's happening in markets right now?" → show you pay attention

### For MBA Candidates
- "What does your MBA add?" → frameworks, network, leadership perspective
- "How do you transition from [previous career]?" → narrative bridge
- "Why not stay in [previous field]?" → genuine pull toward finance, not just push away from old career

## Tips
- Finance interviews are more technical than consulting — know your accounting cold
- Have 1-2 stock pitches ready even if not interviewing for buy-side
- Know recent deals in your target sector (last 90 days)
- For MBA pivots: the "why finance" story matters more than technical perfection
- Practice mental math — quick back-of-envelope calculations impress
- Read the Wall Street Journal daily for 2 weeks before interviews
