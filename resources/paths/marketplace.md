# Uber / DoorDash — Marketplace + Operations + Metrics

## Interview Format
- Typically 4-5 interviews for PM roles
- Heavy emphasis on: metrics, execution, marketplace dynamics
- Often includes a case study or estimation round
- May include a take-home product exercise

## What They Test

### Marketplace Thinking
- "How would you improve rider/driver matching?"
- "A metric improved on one side of the marketplace but hurt the other —
  what do you do?"
- Show you think about multi-sided dynamics:
  supply vs demand, network effects, chicken-and-egg problems
- Understand unit economics: CAC, LTV, take rate, contribution margin

### Metrics & Execution
- "Metric X dropped 15% week over week — walk me through diagnosis"
- Structure: Define scope → Segment (geography, platform, user type) →
  Internal vs external → Hypothesize → Validate → Act
- "How would you measure success for [feature]?"
- Always define: North Star metric, leading indicators, guardrail metrics
- Think about counter-metrics (improving delivery speed vs driver safety)

### Operational Rigor
- "How would you launch [product] in a new city?"
- Think about: local regulations, supply bootstrapping, demand generation,
  competitive dynamics, operational playbooks
- "What would you do if a major outage hit during peak hours?"
- Show you can think about systems at scale with real-world constraints

### Speed & Scrappiness
- Both companies value moving fast with good judgment
- "What's the fastest you've shipped something? What did you sacrifice?"
- "How do you decide when something is 'good enough' to ship?"
- Show you can make 80/20 tradeoffs without being reckless

## Uber-Specific
- Know the cultural norms: "We build globally, we live locally"
- Uber values: Great minds don't think alike, Act like an owner,
  Obsess over the customer
- Think about rides, delivery, freight, and emerging bets
- Show comfort with regulatory and ethical complexity

## DoorDash-Specific
- Know WeDash (all employees do deliveries) — shows operational empathy
- DoorDash values: customer obsession, bias for action, make room at the table
- Think about merchant, dasher, and consumer as three distinct customers
- CEO Tony Xu's "1% done" philosophy — always improving
- Strong operational culture — show you get your hands dirty

## Tips
- Have a marketplace mental model ready (two-sided dynamics, liquidity)
- Practice metric decomposition trees out loud
- Know the competitive landscape (Uber vs Lyft, DoorDash vs Uber Eats vs Instacart)
- Show you can think globally but execute locally
- These roles require both strategic thinking AND operational grit
